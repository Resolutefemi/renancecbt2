Academic Mastery Tips

The Feynman Technique

Named after physicist Richard Feynman, this is the best way to ensure you actually understand a concept instead of just memorizing it.

The 4 Steps:

  • Choose a Concept: Pick a topic (e.g., Newton's Second Law).
  • Teach it to a Child: Write an explanation as if you're teaching someone with no background in the subject. Use simple words.
  • Identify Gaps: When you get stuck or use complex jargon, go back to the source material to simplify that part.
  • Review & Simplify: Refine your explanation until it is brief and clear.

The Forgetting Curve

Humans forget 50% of new information within 24 hours unless it is reviewed. To combat this, use Spaced Repetition.

Review Schedule:

  • 1st Review: Same day
  • 2nd Review: After 24 hours
  • 3rd Review: After 1 week
  • 4th Review: After 1 month

Active Recall:

Instead of re-reading your notes, close the book and try to write down everything you remember from scratch. This strengthens brain connections.

Rapid Learning Techniques

The Pomodoro Method

Study for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After 4 cycles, take a long break (20 mins). This prevents brain fatigue during heavy FUTA courses.

Eat The Frog

Do your hardest subject first (usually MTH 101 or PHY 101) when your energy is highest. Once the "Frog" is eaten, the rest of the day feels easier.
